## Core Problem Statement

Law firms using AI in live legal work have a visibility problem,
not a technology problem. Leadership cannot clearly answer:
- Where is AI deployed in active workflows?
- What review protocols govern AI outputs?
- Who owns accountability when a gap surfaces?

ABA Formal Opinion 512 connects AI use directly to competence,
confidentiality, and supervision obligations. D.C. Bar Opinion 388
is explicit: the record is the defense. Firms without documented
governance are exposed — "I didn't know" is no longer a bar
complaint defense.

Enforcement signal (Q1 2026):
- $145K total AI-related court sanctions
- $109.7K single-attorney penalty (Oregon)
- E&O carriers beginning to ask about AI governance protocols
